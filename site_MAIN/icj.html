LEGAL THRESHOLD METPROVISIONAL MEASURES ORDER I — 26 JAN 2024
On January 26, 2024, the ICJ ruled 15–2 that South Africa had standing to bring the case and that the right of Palestinians in Gaza to be protected from acts of genocide is "plausible." The Court recognized a real and imminent risk of irreparable prejudice to these rights, triggering binding Provisional Measures under international law. Israel was ordered to: (1) take all measures to prevent acts under Genocide Convention Art. II(a)–(d); (2) ensure its military does not commit such acts; (3) prevent and punish direct and public incitement to genocide; (4) preserve all evidence related to allegations under Art. II and III; and (5) report to the Court within one month on measures taken. Only Judge ad hoc Barak and Judge Sebutinde dissented — both on procedural rather than factual grounds. Israel submitted its compliance report on February 23, 2024. The Court assessed it as inadequate. → View Full Order
THRESHOLD ESCALATEDPROVISIONAL MEASURES ORDER II — 28 MAR 2024
On March 28, 2024, the ICJ issued a second, more pointed provisional measures order after concluding that "Palestinians in Gaza are no longer facing only a risk of famine… but that famine is setting in." At least 31 people including 27 children had died of malnutrition and dehydration at the time of the order. The Court found that Israel had failed to comply with the January 26 order — specifically the obligation to ensure unimpeded humanitarian access. The second order mandated that Israel take immediate and effective measures to ensure the unimpeded provision of urgently needed basic services and humanitarian assistance, including food, water, medicine, fuel, and shelter. South Africa filed the request for additional measures on 6 March 2024. Mezan Center for Human Rights (May 2024): "Nearly three months later, Israel has blatantly ignored the provisional measures orders issued by the ICJ." → View Legal Analysis
COMPLIANCE FAILURE DOCUMENTEDPROVISIONAL MEASURES ORDER III — 24 MAY 2024
On May 24, 2024, the Court issued a third provisional measures order following South Africa's urgent request of 10 May 2024 — filed in direct response to Israel's announcement of a ground offensive in Rafah, where approximately 1.4 million displaced civilians were sheltering. The Court found that "the current situation arising from Israel's military offensive in Rafah entails a further risk of irreparable prejudice to the plausible rights claimed by South Africa and that there is urgency." The Court ordered Israel to immediately halt its military offensive in Rafah, keep the Rafah crossing open for humanitarian aid, enable access for investigatory missions, and take effective measures to ensure unrestricted access for UN personnel. Israel did not comply. The Rafah offensive began on May 28, 2024 — four days after the order. The International Court of Justice has now issued three binding provisional measures orders against Israel in this case. Israel has complied with none of them in full. → View Order Summary

Satellite Analysis: Northern Gaza Buffer Zone

High-resolution chronological satellite comparison compiled by UNOSAT (UN Satellite Centre) and independently verified by Corey Scher (CUNY Graduate Center) and Jamon Van Den Hoek (Oregon State University) showing the systematic demolition of residential infrastructure to create a de facto 1–3km exclusion perimeter along the northern Gaza boundary. Analysis through January 2024 estimated that 35,000 structures — approximately 50–70% of all buildings in northern Gaza — had been damaged or destroyed. The Netzarim Corridor, bisecting the territory east-to-west, was identified as a permanent military axis cutting off the north from humanitarian access. Military Directive 1644-C: Land Appropriation

Internal and declassified communications detailing the legal architecture used to facilitate the transfer of Area C territories to civilian administrative control under the guise of military necessity, violating Article 49 of the Fourth Geneva Convention. The mechanism — rooted in a 1967 military order retroactively extending British Mandatory "abandoned property" legislation — has been the operational instrument for confiscating approximately 60% of the West Bank (designated Area C under Oslo II). B'Tselem and Al-Haq have documented over 300 individual land seizure orders issued under this framework since 2000. The ICJ's July 2024 Advisory Opinion found the entire settlement enterprise — which relies on this confiscatory legal framework — to be illegal under international law and ordered its dismantlement.

EXH.108-D

AI Targeting Algorithms: Lavender, The Gospel & Where's Daddy?

Whistleblower testimony from six Israeli intelligence officers, published by +972 Magazine and Local Call (April 2024), verifying the deployment of three automated machine-learning systems with virtually no meaningful human oversight. Lavender: an AI system generating a kill list of up to 37,000 Palestinian "suspects" by identifying patterns of mobile phone use and social associations — with a self-reported 10% false-positive rate, meaning approximately 3,700 misidentified civilians. Officers confirmed reviewing individual Lavender targets for as little as 20 seconds before authorizing lethal strikes. Where's Daddy?: a geolocation tracker purposely designed to bomb targets when they return home at night — maximizing the likelihood of killing entire families. The Gospel: a structural targeting AI accelerating designation of buildings as legitimate military targets, used to justify destruction of apartment complexes, universities, and banks. Democracy Now journalist Yuval Abraham, who broke the story: "AI-based warfare allows people to escape accountability."

EXH.221-H

Hospital Targeting Register: Art. 18 GCIV Breaches

Registry of documented attacks on, and military occupations of, protected medical facilities under Fourth Geneva Convention Art. 18 — compiled by Human Rights Watch, WHO, and MSF. Al-Shifa Medical Complex: besieged twice (November 2023 and March 2024); IDF operated inside the compound; mass graves discovered in the courtyard containing bodies with zip-tied wrists and execution-style wounds following withdrawal. Kamal Adwan Hospital: tank fire on the maternity ward; Director Dr. Hussam Abu Safiya detained by name during an IDF raid on January 27, 2025. Al-Rantisi Pediatric Hospital and Indonesian Hospital: occupied as military outposts; patients abandoned without care. WHO confirmed by January 2024 that 94% of Gaza's hospitals had been damaged or destroyed, and that over 300 attacks on healthcare had been documented since October 7. ICRC: a hospital loses its protected status only if it is used to commit acts harmful to the enemy — and only after a warning has been given and disregarded. No evidence has been produced that any Gaza hospital received formal warning before attack.

EXH.315-F

Engineered Famine: Starvation as a Method of Warfare

Evidentiary file establishing deliberate obstruction of food access as a state policy, constituting a war crime under Protocol I Art. 54 and forming part of the genocidal conduct alleged by South Africa at the ICJ. IPC Integrated Food Security Phase Classification (December 2024): the entire population of Gaza facing acute food insecurity (Phase 3 or above); 1 in 4 facing catastrophic Phase 5 famine conditions — the worst classification. Documented mechanisms: closure of Rafah, Kerem Shalom, and Erez crossings; turning back aid convoys mid-route; destruction of all 11 water and wastewater treatment plants; bulldozing of agricultural land; denial of fishing access; blocking of fuel for water pumps and hospitals. MSF field surgeons performed amputations without general anaesthetic due to blocked medical supply chains. On March 28, 2024, the ICJ found that "famine is setting in" and cited at least 31 deaths from malnutrition — issuing its second provisional measures order specifically addressing the food blockade. The ICC warrant for Netanyahu and Gallant explicitly lists starvation as a method of warfare as the first charge.

EXH.509-B

Forensic Architecture: Shireen Abu Akleh Assassination

Comprehensive ballistic and acoustic reconstruction by Forensic Architecture (commissioned by Al Jazeera) establishing that Al Jazeera veteran correspondent Shireen Abu Akleh was killed on May 11, 2022 in Jenin by a single sniper bullet fired from an identified IDF position — while wearing clearly marked PRESS body armour and helmet with no armed combatants in her vicinity. The report cross-references six independent video reconstructions, acoustic ballistic analysis, GPS trace data, and eyewitness testimony. CNN, AP, the New York Times, and the UN Human Rights Office all independently reached the same conclusion. The US State Department confirmed the bullet came from an IDF position but declined to characterize it as intentional. Israel conducted no prosecution. The killing establishes a documented pattern of direct targeting of clearly identified press personnel — the foundational evidentiary basis for the ICC Media Suppression docket and the pattern of impunity charge in the press blackout archive.

West Bank: State-Facilitated Settler Violence Register
