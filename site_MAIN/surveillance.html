BIOMETRIC OCCUPATION ARCHITECTURE — WOLF ECOSYSTEMSOURCE: AMNESTY INTERNATIONAL // MAY 2023
Amnesty International's May 2023 report "Automated Apartheid" revealed the full architecture of Israel's biometric surveillance network in the West Bank, centered on three interlinked systems: Blue Wolf (mobile harvesting app), Wolf Pack (central biometric database), and Red Wolf (checkpoint facial recognition). Together these systems track every Palestinian civilian without their knowledge or consent:

— Blue Wolf: A smartphone app issued to Israeli soldiers that allows them to photograph Palestinian civilians and instantly cross-reference against Wolf Pack. Military units are ranked weekly by the number of new Palestinian profiles submitted, with commanders awarding prizes — including paid leave — to battalions with the highest scores, effectively gamifying mass biometric data collection from an occupied population.

— Wolf Pack: The central database aggregating all biometric data — photographs, location history, family connections, wanted status, movement restrictions, and permit classifications — compiled exclusively on Palestinians. 2.5M+ profiles logged.

— Red Wolf: Deployed at checkpoints in Hebron's H2 sector (a 20% area of Hebron placed under Israeli military control). Palestinians' faces are scanned — without consent — as they pass through a physical turnstile. A colored light indicator (green, yellow, red) automatically determines passage. The system enrolls any new face it encounters, and will deny passage if no existing profile exists. No human override is provided. AI TARGETING // +972 MAGAZINE INVESTIGATION

Lavender — The Kill-List Machine

+972 Magazine's April 2024 investigation — based on testimonies from six IDF intelligence officers with direct operational involvement — is the most comprehensive public documentation of Lavender to date. The system was developed by IDF Unit 0 and uses machine learning to process surveillance metadata (WhatsApp group memberships, location data, contact networks, social associations) to assign every male Palestinian in Gaza a numerical score indicating probability of Hamas or PIJ affiliation. Early in the war, 37,000 people were flagged. Officers confirmed that for "junior operatives" — low-ranking or peripheral suspects — as little as 20 seconds of human review was required before authorizing a lethal strike. Civilian casualty thresholds of up to 20 non-combatant deaths per "junior" target kill were pre-approved. The system's admitted error rate was 10%. Officers told +972: "The machine did it coldly. It was like there was a factory, and [the work] was just going." A subsequent analysis by AI and surveillance experts in +972 concluded: "AI-based warfare allows people to escape accountability."

AI TARGETING // +972 / LOCAL CALL / GUARDIAN

The Gospel & Where's Daddy — Infrastructure & Family Strikes

The Gospel (Habsora) was developed before October 2023 to automate IDF target generation at scale — moving from hand-curated military target banks to AI-generated target recommendations at 100+ per day. The system specifically classifies civilian infrastructure (apartment blocks, universities, banks, municipal buildings) as valid targets under the doctrine of generating "civil pressure" on Hamas. Gospel provides pre-calculated estimated civilian death counts before each strike — meaning civilian fatalities are anticipated and approved, not incidental. Where's Daddy? operates in the post-Lavender stage: after a target is flagged, the system tracks them geospatially and sends an alert when they are at home with their families. The West Point Lieber Institute's October 2025 legal analysis confirmed this architecture "may be inconsistent with the principles of distinction, proportionality, and precautions in attack" under international humanitarian law, noting that the IDF authorized strikes that "resulted in casualties among family members who were not directly participating in hostilities."

BIOMETRIC OCCUPATION // AMNESTY INTERNATIONAL — MAY 2023

Automated Apartheid — The Wolf Ecosystem

Amnesty International's "Automated Apartheid" (May 2023) is the definitive investigation into Israel's biometric surveillance architecture in the West Bank. The report reveals the three-component Wolf ecosystem — Blue Wolf, Wolf Pack, Red Wolf — as a deliberately integrated architecture for mass biometric population control, deployed exclusively against the Palestinian civilian population. Amnesty documented that Israeli soldiers in Hebron in 2020 described Blue Wolf's gamified collection system: competing to photograph the most Palestinians, with commanders offering prizes including paid leave for top-scoring battalions. Red Wolf, deployed at H2 Hebron checkpoints, scans faces through physical turnstiles and controls access via a traffic-light system — fully automated, without human appeal mechanism. The database (Wolf Pack) contains biometric data for over 2.5 million Palestinians. Amnesty concluded the system constitutes a "tool of apartheid" under international law — automated racial segregation, enforced by algorithm.

CIVIL SOCIETY INFILTRATION // CITIZEN LAB / FRONT LINE DEFENDERS

Pegasus — Targeting Palestinian Defenders

Front Line Defenders' October 2021 investigation — independently confirmed by both the Citizen Lab (University of Toronto) and Amnesty International Security Lab — documented the Pegasus infection of six Palestinian human rights defenders. All worked for civil society organizations based in the West Bank. Four of the six infected devices ran on Israeli SIM cards, directly contradicting NSO Group's public claim that Pegasus cannot target Israeli phone numbers. Three of the targeted defenders worked for organizations that the Israeli Ministry of Defence designated as "terrorist organizations" via classified military orders — a designation simultaneously disputed as groundless by Human Rights Watch, Amnesty International, and the EU. HRW noted that the targeted organizations had also been subjected to prior Israeli state harassment: office raids, equipment confiscation, staff detention, and travel bans. Over 18 human rights organizations issued a joint statement concluding that "this attack is part of a broader assault on Palestinian civil society."

COMMERCIAL SURVEILLANCE // +972 / NPR / MONDOWEISS

AnyVision / Oosto — The Checkpoint Optics Market

AnyVision (now Oosto) is an Israeli facial recognition company whose technology has been deployed at West Bank military checkpoints under the product name "Google Ayosh" — a portmanteau using the Israeli government's term for the West Bank. The system pulls from a 450,000-image database of Palestinian ID photos compiled by the Israeli military to verify workers at crossing points. Microsoft's venture capital arm (M12) invested $74 million in AnyVision in June 2019, generating significant public scrutiny. Microsoft commissioned an independent audit, which found no breach of its stated facial recognition principles. Microsoft then divested in March 2020. Despite divestment, Oosto continues to operate at Israeli checkpoints. The US National Institute of Standards and Technology has found that facial recognition software — including from AnyVision — has elevated error rates for non-Caucasian individuals, raising the risk of false detainment for the Palestinian population the system is deployed to surveil.

Gaza as AI Testing Environment

The Cairo Review's June 2025 analysis identified Gaza as a live operational testing environment for AI warfare systems that Israel subsequently markets globally. AI and surveillance scholar Sophia Goodfriend documented in +972 Magazine that the IDF "is not relying on fully autonomous weapons — rather, intelligence units use AI-powered targeting systems to rank civilians and civilian infrastructure according to their likelihood of being affiliated with militant organizations." The Cairo Review's analysis found that combat-proven AI systems tested in Gaza — including targeting algorithms, drone-integrated facial recognition, and behavioral profiling engines — are being positioned for global export sales, with the designation "battle-tested in Gaza" functioning as a commercial differentiator. This transforms the civilian population of Gaza into a live dataset for commercial product development. The review identified Corsight AI, Oosto, Elbit Systems, and Rafael Advanced Defense Systems as primary commercial beneficiaries of the operational testing cycle.
