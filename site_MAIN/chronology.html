Chronology of Occupation

Pre-Nakba (Prior to 1948)

During the twilight of the Ottoman Empire and the subsequent British Mandatory period, the foundational infrastructure for demographic replacement was established. This era saw the issuance of the Balfour Declaration, the implementation of land transfer regulations, and the violent suppression of indigenous Palestinian general strikes and uprisings.

The Nakba (1948)

The "Catastrophe" marked the mass expulsion of roughly 750,000 Palestinians from their ancestral homes. Zionist militias, and later the newly formed Israeli military, executed a series of military operations resulting in massacres, forced death marches, and the erasure of entire communities to establish a demographic majority.

The Naksa (1967)

Translating to the "Setback," the 1967 Six-Day War resulted in Israel's military occupation of the West Bank, East Jerusalem, and the Gaza Strip. This triggered a second massive wave of displacement, uprooting an estimated 300,000 Palestinians, and marked the beginning of the ongoing military occupation and illegal settlement enterprise in these territories.

First & Second Intifadas (1987-2004)

The First Intifada (1987–1993) was characterized by widespread grassroots civil disobedience, general strikes, and stone-throwing against heavily armed occupation forces, met with a formal "break their bones" policy. Following the failure of the Oslo Accords, the Second Intifada (2000–2005) erupted, resulting in intense armed conflict, extreme military crackdowns, the re-invasion of Palestinian cities, and the commencement of the Separation Wall.
