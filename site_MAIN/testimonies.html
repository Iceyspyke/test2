Verified by BBC / PHRI Date: Feb 15, 2024 Loc: Nasser Hospital, Khan Younis
They separated the doctors from the patients and made us kneel in the courtyard. We were stripped, blindfolded, and loaded into vehicles. For seven days they held me in a facility where muzzled dogs were set upon us. My colleague's hand was deliberately broken. I am a physician. I went to that hospital to heal people. When I came back, the ICU had been emptied.
Dr. Ahmed A. — Volunteer Medical Officer, Nasser Hospital
Affidavit collected by Physicians for Human Rights among 21 medical workers detained during the IDF's February 2024 raid on Nasser Hospital. The BBC independently corroborated accounts of blindfolding, forced stripping, stress positions, and deliberate injury of detained medical staff. WHO reported nearly 200 patients — including six ICU cases — were left without care during the operation.

Verified by Human Rights Watch Date: Jan 22, 2025 Loc: Jenin Refugee Camp, West Bank
On the second day, drones circled the camp and ordered us to leave. I didn't see the drone, but I heard it — 'Evacuate your home. If you don't, you will bear the responsibility. We will destroy the camp.' I walked out with nothing. When I looked back, a bulldozer was already inside my kitchen.
Fatima B. — Jenin Refugee Camp Resident (anonymized per HRW protocol)
Human Rights Watch documented the IDF's systematic eviction of Jenin refugee camp in January 2025, with soldiers using drone-mounted loudspeakers, heavy bulldozers, and helicopter gunfire to displace approximately 14,000 residents. No designated safe exit corridors were provided. Residents were tracked by overhead drones as they fled. The operation was characterized by HRW as a forced displacement constituting a potential crime against humanity under the Rome Statute.
Verified by WHO / UNICEF Date: Nov 2023 — Apr 2024 Loc: Northern Gaza (Compiled Field Record)
We drink water that smells foul. My children constantly complain of stomach aches. We don't even have clean water to wash their wounds. Every scrape becomes a potential infection. Do we let them drink this water or let them stay thirsty? These are not choices. They are punishments.
Multiple Civilian Witnesses — Structured Interview Cohort (PMC 2025)
Compiled from structured clinical interviews published in the Peer-reviewed PMC journal (2025). WHO confirmed that by October 2024, more than 80% of Gaza's water and sanitation infrastructure had been destroyed, with over one million people receiving less than 15 litres of water per day — below the minimum emergency survival threshold set by Sphere Guidelines. 96% of Gaza's water is classified undrinkable by UNICEF. No functional wastewater treatment plants remained operational.
Verified by MSF Date: Jun 08, 2024 Loc: Al-Nuseirat Refugee Camp, Central Gaza
I lost neighbors in those strikes. People I had known my entire life. We were operating at the camp clinic when the bombs hit the residential block three streets over. By nightfall, the dead included people who had come to us for vaccines the week before. There is no sanctuary left. Not a clinic, not a school, not a home.
Dr. Hazem M. — Palestinian MSF Physician
Testimony published directly by Médecins Sans Frontières following the strikes of June 8, 2024, in which Israeli forces attacked Al-Nuseirat refugee camp during a military operation to retrieve hostages. MSF's Dr. Hazem Maloh documented the deaths of friends and neighbors in coordinated strikes across a densely populated civilian area. MSF condemned the attacks, noting that strikes on civilian infrastructure in an active humanitarian zone constitute a violation of international humanitarian law.
Verified by Amnesty International Date: Apr 14, 2024 Loc: Al-Mughayyir, Ramallah District
They came at midday in a convoy — settlers and soldiers together. You could not tell them apart. They set fire to the cars first, then the outbuildings. When we tried to call the emergency line, a soldier waved us back with his rifle. There were children in those houses. They burned our trees — trees my grandfather planted — while the soldiers watched and did nothing.
Anonymous Witness — Local Resident (testimony to Amnesty field researchers)
Affidavit filed as part of Amnesty International's April 2024 investigation into state-backed settler violence in the occupied West Bank. Between April 12–16, 2024, hundreds of settlers launched coordinated raids on Palestinian villages following the death of a settler teenager. Four Palestinians were killed, including 17-year-old Omar Hamed. The UN recorded more than 700 settler attacks between October 7, 2023 and April 2024, with IDF soldiers in uniform present in nearly half of the incidents. Human Rights Watch documented that the IDF either participated in or actively failed to prevent attacks in 20 communities.
Verified by PCHR / Anadolu Agency Date: Jan 10, 2025 Loc: Al-Nuseirat Camp, Central Gaza
We were covering the rescue operation, clearly wearing press vests. Saed was filming the paramedics when the sniper fired. The bullet entered his back and came out his chest. He was still holding his camera when he fell. There was an Israeli sniper on the rooftop directly above us. He could see we were journalists. He fired anyway.
Khamis R. — Freelance Journalist, Reuters / Al Jazeera (eyewitness)
Sworn testimony filed with the Palestinian Centre for Human Rights following the January 10, 2025 killing of photojournalist Saed Abu Nabhan (26), correspondent for Al-Ghad TV and Anadolu Agency. Abu Nabhan was shot by an IDF sniper positioned on a high residential building while covering rescue operations. PCHR confirmed journalists were wearing distinctive uniforms in an area known to IDF. A paramedic at the scene independently corroborated the targeted nature of the shot. PCHR characterised the killing as part of a deliberate pattern of targeting press personnel to suppress documentation of military operations.
