Active Legal Proceedings

International judicial actions currently active at the International Criminal Court (ICC) and International Court of Justice (ICJ). These proceedings constitute the first time in history that a sitting head of government has faced active ICC arrest warrants while in office.

Proceeding I — ICC
Arrest Warrants: Netanyahu & Gallant

On 21 November 2024, ICC Pre-Trial Chamber I issued arrest warrants for Benjamin Netanyahu (Prime Minister) and Yoav Gallant (former Defence Minister) for war crimes and crimes against humanity committed from 8 October 2023 to at least 20 May 2024. Charges include: the war crime of starvation as a method of warfare; and the crimes against humanity of murder, persecution, and other inhumane acts. The Chamber found reasonable grounds to believe both individuals bear criminal responsibility as co-perpetrators. All 124 ICC member states are obligated under the Rome Statute to arrest and surrender the defendants if found on their territory.

View ICC Arrest Warrant
Proceeding II — ICJ
South Africa v. Israel (Genocide Convention)

South Africa filed its application on 29 December 2023 alleging Israel is violating the Genocide Convention in Gaza. On 26 January 2024, the ICJ accepted prima facie jurisdiction and ordered Israel to prevent genocidal acts, allow humanitarian aid, and prevent incitement to genocide. On 28 March 2024, the Court ordered additional measures requiring Israel to ensure basic food supplies reach Gaza. On 24 May 2024, the Court (13–2) ordered Israel to immediately halt its offensive in Rafah. Israel has been formally cited for non-compliance with all three provisional measures orders.

View ICJ Case Record
Proceeding III — ICJ
ICJ Advisory Opinion: Occupation

On 19 July 2024, the ICJ issued a landmark Advisory Opinion ruling Israel's occupation of the West Bank, East Jerusalem, and Gaza Strip unlawful under international law, requiring the complete and unconditional withdrawal of Israeli forces. The court found that Israel's continued presence violates the right to self-determination of the Palestinian people, the prohibition of acquisition of territory by force, and various obligations under international humanitarian law. It imposed obligations on all states not to aid or assist in maintaining the unlawful occupation.

Legal Standards Reference

International Law // Genocide Convention

Article II: Genocide

Acts committed with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial or religious group. Includes killing members, causing serious bodily or mental harm, and deliberately inflicting conditions of life calculated to bring about physical destruction.

International Law // Rome Statute

Article 7: Crimes Against Humanity

Specific acts committed as part of a widespread or systematic attack directed against any civilian population, with knowledge of the attack. Includes murder, extermination, enslavement, deportation, and forcible transfer.

International Law // Apartheid Convention

Article II: Apartheid

Inhuman acts committed for the purpose of establishing and maintaining domination by one racial group of persons over any other racial group of persons and systematically oppressing them.

International Law // Rome Statute

Article 8: War Crimes

Grave breaches of the Geneva Conventions including wilful killing, torture, inhumane treatment, extensive destruction of property not justified by military necessity, and intentionally directing attacks against civilians, hospitals, and protected personnel. Applicable to acts committed in international armed conflict and forms the basis of the ICC's charges against Netanyahu and Gallant.

International Law // Geneva Convention IV

Civilian Protection in Occupation

The Fourth Geneva Convention (1949) requires an occupying power to ensure the welfare of the civilian population, prohibit collective punishment, and maintain access to food, medicine, and medical care. It prohibits the destruction of property not imperatively demanded by military operations. The ICJ July 2024 Advisory Opinion found Israel in systematic breach of these obligations.

International Law // Additional Protocol I

Article 54: Starvation as a Weapon

Prohibits attacking, destroying, removing, or rendering useless objects indispensable to the survival of the civilian population — including foodstuffs, agricultural areas, crops, livestock, drinking water installations, and irrigation works. The use of starvation of civilians as a method of warfare is a war crime under Article 8(2)(b)(xxv) of the Rome Statute and the explicit basis of the ICC charges against Netanyahu and Gallant.
