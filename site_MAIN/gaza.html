History & Siege Context

The Gaza Strip has historically been a focal point of resistance and extreme structural violence. Following the 1948 Nakba, Gaza absorbed hundreds of thousands of displaced refugees, drastically altering its demographics. It fell under Egyptian administration until the 1967 Naksa, when Israel occupied the territory militarily.

In 2005, Israel unilaterally dismantled its settlements but maintained absolute control over Gaza's borders, airspace, and maritime access. Following 2007, a comprehensive land, air, and sea blockade was imposed, severely restricting movement and essential goods. Successive military bombardments have devastated civil infrastructure, generating an immense toll on human life.

The Humanitarian Blockade

Since 2007, the Gaza Strip has been under a comprehensive siege. The severing of water, electricity, and telecommunication lines, coupled with severe restrictions on the entry of medical supplies and food quotas, has precipitated a manufactured epidemiological risk and absolute systemic collapse.
