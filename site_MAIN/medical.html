Human Rights Watch's March 2025 report "War Crimes While Occupying Hospitals" documented that Israeli military forces caused deaths and unnecessary suffering of Palestinian patients during hospital occupations across Gaza. Key findings across the full campaign:

— 94% of Gaza's hospitals have been damaged or destroyed (OHCHR, Dec 2025), leaving the entire civilian population without functional healthcare infrastructure.
— At Kamal Adwan Hospital, Israeli forces surrounded the facility on December 11, 2023. Tank fire hit the maternity department on December 11, killing two women. By December 17, at least 8 patients had died including a 9-year-old child (WHO Director-General). All males aged 16 and over were screened on-site; scores of healthcare workers were arrested. Director Dr. Hussam Abu Safiya was detained in a raid on December 27, 2024, and his body remains withheld.
— At Al-Shifa, WHO described the complex as "an empty shell with human graves." Forensic Architecture and the Palestinian Civil Defence documented the extraction of 381+ bodies from mass graves inside and around the compound — multiple bearing signs of restraint (zip-tied hands), execution-style wounds, and live burial. Amnesty International stated: "Mass grave sites are potential crime scenes." The UN High Commissioner for Human Rights said they were "horrified."
— HRW concluded: Israeli military forces committed war crimes during hospital occupations. Under IHL, intentional attacks against medical facilities, personnel, and patients protected under Geneva Convention IV constitute grave breaches — war crimes carrying individual criminal responsibility. DECRYPTED: POLIO EPIDEMIC — FIRST OUTBREAK IN 25 YEARSSOURCE: WHO / UNICEF / GPLN // 2024–2025
On July 16, 2024, the Global Polio Laboratory Network (GPLN) notified the detection of six circulating variant poliovirus type 2 (cVDPV2) isolates in environmental wastewater samples collected from Deir al-Balah and Khan Younis. Gaza's health ministry officially declared an epidemic on July 29, 2024 — the first confirmed poliovirus case in Gaza in 25 years, following the complete eradication of the virus from the region in 1999.

The first confirmed clinical case was a 10-month-old child in August 2024. WHO, UNICEF, UNRWA and the Palestinian Ministry of Health jointly launched an emergency mass vaccination campaign from September 1–12, 2024, successfully vaccinating 559,161 children (95% target coverage), requiring negotiated tactical pauses in active combat operations. A second round was conducted October–November 2024. A third campaign (February 22–26, 2025) vaccinated 603,000 children following the detection of continued poliovirus circulation in wastewater samples.

Root cause: Total destruction of Gaza's wastewater treatment infrastructure has resulted in raw sewage mixing with groundwater and flooding displacement camp sites, creating ideal conditions for fecal-oral transmission. Vaccine supply blockades delayed initial immunization response by weeks. WHO and PubMed peer review (February 2025) confirmed the outbreak is linked to strains circulating in Egypt, consistent with cross-border transmission facilitated by displacement conditions. OHCHR (December 2025) reported: women in Gaza are three times more likely to die in childbirth and three times more likely to miscarry compared with pre-war levels. A 300% increase in miscarriage rates was recorded by May 2024 (UN expert report), driven by extreme malnutrition, chronic stress, and the obliteration of obstetric infrastructure. As of January 2025, emergency obstetric and newborn care is available at only 7 of 18 partially functional hospitals, and 4 of 11 field hospitals — against a need serving 50,000 pregnant women, including 5,500 imminently due.

Emergency deliveries are performed in overcrowded tents with no sterile equipment, no anaesthesia, and no postoperative care. UNFPA documentation (October 2024): doctors now routinely schedule early C-sections because patients fear future loss of access to surgical care. Nurses report no longer seeing healthy-weight newborns. Newborn deaths have risen sharply. In December 2024, the destruction of Gaza's largest IVF centre eliminated 5,000 embryos. 4GenderJustice's February 2025 legal analysis classifies the documented pattern — systematic destruction of reproductive healthcare infrastructure, blocking of prenatal nutrition, targeting of maternity wards — as reproductive violence constituting crimes against humanity under international law. Facility Destroyed

Al-Shifa Medical Complex

Gaza's largest hospital — the backbone of its health system — subjected to two military sieges (November 2023, March 2024). Following the second two-week siege, WHO Director-General Tedros Adhanom Ghebreyesus confirmed: "Al-Shifa is now an empty shell with human graves." Palestinian Civil Defence exhumed 381+ bodies from mass grave sites inside and adjacent to the compound. Multiple bodies found with zip-tied wrists, execution-style gunshot wounds, and signs of live burial. A WHO-led mission found numerous shallow graves outside the emergency department, with limbs visible above ground. UN High Commissioner and Amnesty International called for immediate international forensic investigation. Forensic Architecture confirmed Israeli forces desecrated pre-existing makeshift burial grounds using military bulldozers.

Facility Destroyed

Nasser Hospital

The primary medical center serving southern Gaza — raided, evacuated at gunpoint, and rendered entirely non-functional. After Israeli forces withdrew, Palestinian Civil Defence discovered mass grave sites on the hospital grounds containing bodies of patients and civilians. Over 392 bodies were extracted across Nasser and Al-Shifa combined, with Al Jazeera reporting signs of torture, execution, and burial of individuals alive. Amnesty International stated the mass graves at Nasser constitute a "potential crime scene" and demanded international forensic access. BBC confirmed UN rights chief was "horrified." Medical Assistance for Palestinians (MAP) documented that some bodies had been "badly disfigured" and zip-tied, raising specific concerns about extrajudicial execution.

Personnel Targeted

Ambulance Fleet Decimation

Documentation of precision strikes against Palestine Red Crescent Society (PRCS) ambulances bearing clearly marked Red Crescent insignia and operating on routes with military clearance from COGAT. WHO and PRCS have logged multiple confirmed strikes against emergency response vehicles. Paramedics killed while responding to active distress calls. Forensic investigation by Fault Lines and Forensic Architecture confirmed satellite imagery showing Israeli military vehicles positioned at strike sites during multiple PRCS ambulance attacks. The systematic targeting of clearly marked emergency vehicles constitutes a grave breach of the Geneva Conventions, which provide absolute protected status to medical transport in armed conflict.

Epidemiological Crisis

Polio & Infectious Disease

On July 16, 2024, the Global Polio Laboratory Network detected cVDPV2 in six wastewater samples from Deir al-Balah and Khan Younis. Gaza officially declared a polio epidemic on July 29, 2024 — the first confirmed poliovirus in Gaza in 25 years. The first clinical case was a 10-month-old child in August 2024. Root cause: complete destruction of wastewater treatment infrastructure generating raw sewage flooding across displacement sites. WHO, UNICEF, and UNRWA launched three emergency mass vaccination campaigns (September 2024, October 2024, February 2025), each requiring negotiated combat pauses. Third campaign reached 603,000 children. WHO confirmed poliovirus continues to circulate in wastewater as of February 2025, indicating the outbreak remains active.

Neonatal Collapse

Incubator Severance

Verified forensic logs of neonatal intensive care units (NICUs) where premature infants perished following the forced, gunpoint evacuation of medical staff and intentional severance of backup oxygen and power supply. WHO confirmed at least 20 patients — including NICU patients — died at Al-Shifa during the March 2024 siege due to restricted movement of medics and severance of care. WHO cited the acting hospital director: patients were held "in abysmal conditions" with no access to treatment or evacuation. As of January 2025, only 7 of 18 partially functional hospitals have any emergency obstetric or newborn care capacity. OHCHR: newborn mortality has increased sharply compared to pre-war baseline. Gaza's largest IVF centre was destroyed in December 2024, eliminating 5,000 embryos.

Medical Brain-Drain

Targeted Specialist Attrition

Dr. Adnan Al-Bursh — head of orthopaedics at Al-Shifa Hospital, former medical adviser to the Palestinian national football team, widely described as "the safety valve" of Gaza's orthopaedic care system — was detained by name by Israeli forces during the December 2023 Al-Awda Hospital raid while actively treating patients. He was transferred to Ofer Prison in the occupied West Bank. On April 19, 2024, Palestinian prisoner associations confirmed his death. The cause: torture, per a joint statement by Palestinian prisoner advocacy groups. He was 50 years old. His body was withheld. An Israeli military spokesperson cited "national security" without detailing the cause of death. He was one of at least several senior medical specialists detained, killed, or disappeared in custody during the conflict, resulting in permanent institutional medical expertise loss from which Gaza's healthcare system cannot recover.

Psychological Impact

Generational PTSD Matrix

WHO and UNICEF epidemiological assessments document severe, multi-generational psychological damage across Gaza's population. Children who have survived sustained aerial bombardment, witnessed the deaths of family members, and been displaced multiple times show acute and complex PTSD, developmental regression, anxiety disorders, and behavioral disintegration — a pattern WHO clinicians have described as the most concentrated child trauma profile they have documented in any active conflict. With no functional psychiatric or psychological infrastructure remaining in Gaza, no specialist care is available. The psychological damage is compounding: each additional escalation, displacement, and family death deepens the clinical picture. MSF field teams report treating children who have entirely stopped speaking. Long-term epidemiological modeling indicates the cognitive and psychological sequelae will extend across at least one generation of Gazan children.

Facility Destroyed

Kamal Adwan Hospital

The last functional hospital serving northern Gaza's 75,000 remaining civilians. Israeli forces surrounded Kamal Adwan on December 11, 2023. Tank fire hit the maternity department that day, killing two women. By December 17, eight patients had died including a 9-year-old child (WHO Director-General). All male hospital personnel aged 16+ were screened and scores arrested. On December 27, 2024, Israeli forces raided the hospital: director Dr. Hussam Abu Safiya was detained by name and transferred to Sde Teiman detention camp. Soldiers rounded up remaining staff, forced them to remove their clothes, and set fire to the building. WHO lost all contact with Abu Safiya from the moment of raid. His wife told +972 Magazine: "The Israeli army deliberately targeted my husband." WHO declared: the hospital is now "out of service," leaving northern Gaza without any functional medical facility.

Supply Blockade

Anesthesia & Analgesic Ban

MSF and WHO have documented the systematic blocking of critical medical supplies at Israeli-controlled entry points into Gaza, resulting in near-total depletion of anesthesia, analgesics, antiseptics, sterile surgical supplies, and blood products. MSF field surgeons operating in Gaza have publicly described performing major amputations — including limb amputations on children — without general anesthetic, using only local anesthetic injections insufficient for the scale of procedures required, or in some cases none at all. OHCHR confirmed the Israeli blockade "prevented the entry of objects indispensable to the survival of civilians, including medical supplies." Under IHL, deliberate denial of medical supplies to a civilian population is prohibited under GCIV Article 23. The 4GenderJustice legal analysis (2025) characterizes the systematic blocking of obstetric medical supplies as a form of reproductive violence constituting crimes against humanity.

Maternal Health

Obstetric System Collapse

OHCHR (December 2025): women in Gaza are three times more likely to die in childbirth and three times more likely to miscarry versus pre-war levels. UN experts recorded a 300% increase in miscarriage rates by May 2024, driven by severe malnutrition, extreme stress, and healthcare infrastructure destruction. UNFPA documents emergency deliveries in overcrowded tents without sterile equipment, with no anesthesia, no postoperative care, and no newborn monitoring. Doctors now schedule early C-sections because patients fear losing access to surgical care before full term. Nurses report no longer seeing healthy-weight newborns. Gaza's largest IVF center was destroyed in December 2024, eliminating 5,000 embryos. 4GenderJustice (February 2025): the documented pattern of systematic destruction of reproductive healthcare constitutes reproductive violence amounting to crimes against humanity under international law.

Personnel Targeted

Medical Staff Mass Detention

HRW's March 2025 investigation confirmed a systematic pattern: during every major hospital siege, Israeli forces screened all males aged 16 and older, arrested scores of healthcare workers, and removed them to undisclosed detention facilities. At Kamal Adwan, Dr. Hussam Abu Safiya — hospital director — was taken by name. At Al-Awda Hospital, Dr. Adnan Al-Bursh was called out by name and forcibly taken while treating patients. Multiple testimonies describe medical staff stripped of clothing in hospital courtyards before transfer. At least two detained doctors — Dr. Al-Bursh and Ismail Abdul Bari Khader — died in custody, with Palestinian prisoner associations confirming torture as the cause of death. The body of Dr. Al-Bursh was withheld following his death on April 19, 2024. HRW classified the targeting, mass detention, and abuse of healthcare personnel as war crimes under international humanitarian law.

Facility Destroyed

Al-Rantisi Pediatric Hospital

The specialized pediatric oncology and children's hospital in Gaza City — forcibly evacuated and subsequently used as an Israeli military outpost and detention and interrogation facility before being subjected to devastating structural damage, halting all cancer treatment for children. MSF and WHO documented that pediatric oncology patients requiring chemotherapy, radiation, and post-surgical care have been denied treatment since the hospital's seizure and military occupation. Under IHL, the use of a protected medical facility as a military base strips it of its protected status under GCIV — but this does not retroactively justify the cessation of life-saving pediatric cancer treatment as a consequence of an occupying force's own violation of humanitarian law.

Facility Destroyed

Indonesian Hospital Siege

The primary medical facility serving northern Gaza — subjected to direct artillery and sniper fire, resulting in patient deaths inside the wards and the total cessation of surgical capacity. Following the collapse of Kamal Adwan Hospital on December 27, 2024, WHO confirmed that the remaining 15 critical patients, 50 caregivers, and 20 health workers from Kamal Adwan were transferred to Indonesian Hospital — despite WHO stating the facility "lacks the necessary equipment and supplies to provide adequate care" for such patients. WHO assessed: "The movement and treatment of these critical patients under such conditions pose grave risks to their survival." The sequential collapse of northern Gaza's hospital network — Indonesian, then Kamal Adwan — left the region's entire civilian population without any functioning medical infrastructure.

Emergency Dispatch

PRCS Dispatch: Hind Rajab

On January 29, 2024, six-year-old Hind Rajab was trapped alive in a car with the bodies of killed family members in Gaza City, calling PRCS dispatchers for help. PRCS obtained explicit military clearance from COGAT to dispatch an ambulance. Paramedics Yusuf al-Zeino and Ahmed al-Madhoun were dispatched from Al-Ahli Hospital, reached Hind's location at approximately 6:00 PM, and were immediately shot at upon arrival. All contact was lost. The last sound on the dispatch call was an explosion. Forensic Architecture's investigation — corroborated by the Washington Post, six independent munitions experts, and satellite imagery analysis — confirmed that Israeli military vehicles were present at the site at the time of the attack, the bullet holes on the ambulance matched ordnance used by Israeli tanks, and the trajectory of the fatal projectile matched the position of nearby Israeli armor. The ambulance had been granted formal COGAT clearance. Hind Rajab and both paramedics were killed.
