SECTOR: HEALTHCARE

Hospital Network Destruction

WHO recorded 697 attacks on healthcare facilities between October 2023 and May 2025. 94% of all hospitals in the Gaza Strip are damaged or destroyed. As of May 2025, only 19 of 36 hospitals remain partially operational — 12 providing varied services, the rest offering basic emergency care only. Al-Shifa Medical Complex, Gaza's largest hospital, was attacked, occupied, and reduced to rubble. The WHO assessment identified a destructive cycle: hospitals are rehabilitated, resupplied, then attacked again.

View WHO Report
SECTOR: WATER & SANITATION

Total WASH Network Collapse

Oxfam and UNICEF confirmed that over 80% of water and sanitation infrastructure across Gaza has been partially or entirely destroyed. All six major wastewater treatment plants are non-functional. 85 out of 103 desalination plants are damaged or destroyed. 73 of 84 sewage pumping stations are destroyed. 1,675 km of Gaza's 4,800 km water and sanitation pipeline network has been obliterated. North Gaza has access to less than 7% of pre-conflict water levels — 5.7 litres per person per day against the WHO minimum of 50 litres. Untreated sewage pools in streets adjacent to displacement shelters. 88% of environmental polio samples across Gaza returned positive.

View Oxfam Report
SECTOR: ROAD NETWORK

Road Infrastructure Erasure

UNOSAT's August 2024 road damage assessment identified approximately 1,190 km of destroyed roads, 415 km of severely affected roads, and 1,440 km of moderately affected roads. Approximately 68% of the total road network has been damaged across the Gaza Strip. The systematic targeting of road corridors was assessed as specifically intended to impede aid delivery, disable emergency vehicle access, and prevent civilian evacuation from active strike zones.

View UNOSAT Assessment
SECTOR: AGRICULTURE

Cropland & Orchard Destruction

FAO-UNOSAT analysis confirmed that 87% of Gaza's total cropland has been damaged as of late September 2025. Orchards and tree crops reached 89% damage; olive orchards — the most culturally and economically significant — sustained 90% destruction. Greenhouses stand at 80% damaged. North Gaza and Gaza City recorded 94% and 91% cropland damage respectively, constituting near-total agricultural erasure. 98% of Gaza's tree cropland was destroyed by October 2025. Over two-thirds of agricultural wells (1,531 in total) are non-functional. Without urgent intervention, FAO warns the entire Gaza Strip risks permanent food production incapacity.

View FAO Report
SECTOR: MARITIME / FISHING

Fishing Fleet & Port Destruction

Forensic Architecture's December 2024 analysis documented the systematic destruction of Gaza's seaport and fishing fleet from October 2023 onward. Israeli naval and air forces destroyed 95% of Palestinian fishermen's assets — including all major fishing docks, 300 small boats, 80 large boats, ice factories, fish markets, and the main wholesale trading hub. Fishing activity now stands at less than 10% of pre-war levels. The fishing sector — the primary protein source for over two million residents — has been eliminated, depriving over 6,000 people of their livelihoods and generating an estimated $7 million per month in indirect losses.

View Forensic Architecture Report
SECTOR: HOUSING

Residential Erasure

UNOSAT confirmed 320,622 housing units damaged as of October 2025 — a 12% increase from July 2025 alone, indicating continued active residential targeting during ceasefire periods. The Governorates of Gaza City and North Gaza recorded the highest density of newly affected structures. Housing accounts for 72% of the World Bank's $18.5 billion total damage estimate, with residential reconstruction alone requiring $15.2 billion. The World Bank assessment found that damage to structures affects every sector of the economy, with no functioning residential zone remaining in North Gaza.

View UNOSAT Report
SECTOR: RUBBLE & RECONSTRUCTION

Rubble Crisis & Rebuild Timeline

The UN estimates 55 million tons of rubble now covers the Gaza Strip — equivalent to filling 13 Great Pyramids of Giza. UNDP has removed approximately 81,000 tons to date, a fraction of the total. Full rubble clearance alone is projected to take over a decade. Total reconstruction costs are estimated by the UN at $70 billion, with the World Bank's joint assessment placing structural damage alone at $18.5 billion — equivalent to 97% of the pre-conflict combined GDP of the West Bank and Gaza. Researchers at leading international institutions project a minimum of ten years for extensive reconstruction, contingent on sustained access, ceasefire adherence, and a broad international financing coalition.

UNDP — FULL RECONSTRUCTION ESTIMATEOct 14, 2025
UNDP Special Representative Jaco Cilliers confirmed the full reconstruction cost of the Gaza Strip is estimated at $70 billion — nearly four times the January 2024 interim assessment. "The destruction in Gaza was almost beyond comprehension," Cilliers stated. 55 million tons of rubble — enough to fill 13 Great Pyramids of Giza — must be cleared before reconstruction can begin. UNDP has removed approximately 81,000 tons to date, reusing over 13,000 tons to pave emergency roads and shelter flooring. At current rates, rubble clearance alone is projected to take over a decade. The Governorates of Gaza City and North Gaza experienced the largest increases in newly affected structures (4,243 and 1,442 respectively).
