FAMINE DECLARATIONSOURCE: WHO / FAO / WFP / UNICEF — AUG 22, 2025
On 22 August 2025, WHO, FAO, WFP, and UNICEF jointly confirmed famine in Gaza for the first time — confirming what UN experts had warned since late 2023. As of September 2025, more than 640,000 people were projected to face IPC Phase 5 (Catastrophe) conditions, marked by starvation, destitution, and preventable death. An additional 1.14 million are in Emergency (IPC Phase 4) and 396,000 in Crisis (IPC Phase 3). 22% of Gaza's entire population — one in five people — is at risk of starvation. Conditions in North Gaza are assessed as "as severe — or worse" than Gaza City, where famine was formally confirmed. The UN Commission of Inquiry concluded these conditions are "the foreseeable and intentional result of Israel's policies," not collateral effects of combat. WHO Famine Confirmation →

Gisha stated: "The official goal of the policy was to wage economic warfare which would paralyze Gaza's economy and, according to the Defence Ministry, create pressure on the Hamas government." The document was in force during the most restrictive years of the blockade (2007–2010), during which the Israeli government simultaneously maintained a classified list of foods permitted or prohibited from entering the territory. DIR-2007-01 // COGAT

Dual-Use List Restrictions

Beginning in 2008, COGAT published a classified list of goods banned from entering Gaza under the designation "dual-use" — items theoretically capable of military application. The list included construction materials, agricultural fertilizers, certain chemicals, optic equipment, water pumps, sanitation materials, communications equipment, and medical devices. CNN's March 2024 investigation documented consistent denials of anesthetics, oxygen, crutches, X-ray machines, water purification tablets, maternity kits, cancer medications, and sleeping bags (rejected for being green — "military association"). The Guardian's 2024 investigation traced blockades on toys, spices, sewing machines, notebooks, industrial salt, and fishing ropes. Physicians for Human Rights confirmed surgical instruments, sterilization equipment, fetal dopplers, and electric wheelchairs as controlled items. The ScienceDirect analysis found the list was structured with deliberately broad categorical language — "Equipment and tools of physical and chemical analysis" — ensuring that thousands of essential civilian items fell under unannounced prohibition. A peer-reviewed study in The Lancet described it as "duality as a weapon of war."

MEMO-2010 // Ministry of Defense

Caloric Intake Formulas

A 2008 Israeli Defence Ministry document — released under court order following Gisha's 3.5-year legal battle — explicitly calculated the daily caloric requirements of Gaza's civilian population to establish a precise minimum threshold for food entry. The document sets 2,279 calories per person per day as the "Red Line" — the WHO minimum floor — not as a humanitarian target, but as the enforced upper limit on food allowed in. Gisha documented that during the blockade's peak years (2007–2010), COGAT maintained a list of foods specifically permitted or prohibited from entering the territory. The Israeli Defense Ministry defended the document by claiming it was intended to "prevent the development of malnutrition" rather than engineer it — a claim directly contradicted by the internal memo's own language, which frames the calculation as part of a broader policy of "economic warfare" to destabilize the Hamas government via civilian deprivation.

NAV-2023 // Maritime Command

Nautical Mile Reductions

The Israeli-Palestinian Interim Agreement (Oslo II, 1995) explicitly guaranteed Palestinian fishermen access to a fishing zone of 20 nautical miles from the Gaza shore. This right has never been honored. Since October 2000, Israel has progressively shrunk the permitted zone: first to 12nm, then to 6nm, then to 3nm, occasionally extending it as a bargaining chip in ceasefire negotiations — only to revoke access when rocket fire resumed. Israeli naval forces enforced these limits via live-fire warnings, arrest, boat seizure, and confiscation of gear. Al Jazeera documented that in April 2019 the zone was reduced from 15nm back to 6nm, citing a single rocket attack. Legal scholars at the Leiden Journal of International Law confirmed the zone was never lawfully contracted — the Oslo maximum of 20nm itself represents only 10% of the 200nm Exclusive Economic Zone to which a coastal state is entitled under international law. Al Mezan documented the steady destruction of Gaza's fishing sector as a direct consequence of these enforced nautical reductions.

INF-2024 // Energy Sector

Grid Severance Protocols

Gaza's sole electricity generating plant was rendered non-operational within the first week of bombardment in October 2023, when Israeli authorities simultaneously halted all fuel deliveries. The World Bank's April 2024 joint damage assessment confirmed a total power grid collapse, classifying the blackout as a contributing factor to cascading failures across water treatment, hospital generators, refrigeration of medicines, and sewage pumping. OCHA and WHO documented that hospitals operated on generator fuel — typically supplied through the Kerem Shalom crossing — for over a year, with supply subject to the same crossing restrictions that blocked food and medicine. Solar arrays, installed as an emergency alternative to the grid collapse, were then systematically destroyed by airstrikes, as confirmed by the World Bank assessment. The UN Commission of Inquiry described the energy blackout as a deliberate act of collective punishment under Article 33 of the Fourth Geneva Convention.

COMM-2018 // Telecommunications

Frequency Jamming Directives

From 2008 onward, COGAT systematically blocked the importation of 3G equipment into Gaza, preventing the territory from upgrading its mobile infrastructure for over a decade. 4G connectivity was not approved until 2021 — 13 years after commercial 4G deployment became standard globally. Access Now's documentation of Gaza's internet outages confirmed repeated, deliberate, total communication blackouts timed to coincide with ground incursions — ensuring no unauthorized transmission of events could occur during maximum violence. As of October 2023, complete blackouts severed all civilian communication infrastructure. The OCHA monitoring of Kerem Shalom crossing documented that telecommunications equipment — classified under the dual-use list — remains one of the most consistently denied aid categories. Systematic electromagnetic jamming of protest sites was documented by the UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission during the Great March of Return.

MED-2021 // Health Sector

Exit Permit Denial Ledgers

The first peer-reviewed population-based study on permit denial mortality (The Lancet, 2021) found that cancer patients whose exit permit applications were denied or delayed faced a death risk 1.45 times higher than those whose permits were approved. The approval rate for patient exit permits dropped from 94% in 2012 to 54% in 2017. Almost a third of all permit applicants are cancer patients — others require surgeries, cardiology, or diagnostic imaging unavailable in Gaza. WHO confirmed that as of November 2025, over 11,000 cancer patients in Gaza require medical evacuation outside, with many awaiting Israeli approval. Between January and October 2025, only 594 patients were evacuated. Physicians for Human Rights documented that in 2008 alone, over 40 patients with cancer and cardiac conditions died after being refused permission to leave through Erez — permits withheld for unspecified "security reasons" even when hospitals in Israel, the West Bank, Jordan, or Egypt had already accepted them.

WTR-2023 // Resource Control

Desalination Fuel Embargo

Gaza's water desalination system depends on continuous fuel supply to power pumps, treatment facilities, and distribution networks. OCHA and Oxfam documented that the fuel blockade — enforced through the Kerem Shalom crossing — specifically and disproportionately targeted water infrastructure. North Gaza was confirmed by Oxfam in February 2025 to be receiving less than 7% of pre-conflict water levels — 5.7 litres per person per day, against the WHO minimum standard of 50 litres. All six major wastewater treatment plants are non-functional. 85 of 103 desalination plants are damaged or destroyed. HRW's November 2024 report found that the combination of the fuel embargo, direct infrastructure destruction, and import blockades on water purification chemicals constituted intentional engineering of drought conditions in a civilian population, not collateral damage. The COGAT dual-use list explicitly includes water pumps and sanitation chemicals as controlled items.

AGR-2015 // Land Expropriation

Lethal Buffer Zones

Israel established a "buffer zone" on the Gazan side of the perimeter fence — an unmarked, variable-width strip enforced by lethal standing orders to shoot any person entering the area. Al Mezan and OCHA documented that the zones effectively annexed between 17–35% of Gaza's total agricultural land, varying in width from 150m to over 1,000m from the fence. FAO confirmed that 87% of Gaza's total cropland has been damaged by 2025, with the buffer zone's expansion during ground operations progressively consuming farmland, irrigation systems, and greenhouse infrastructure. The zones were enforced not through legal declaration but through live-fire orders, meaning no farmer could know in real time where the moving boundary stood. The Great March of Return protests (2018–2019), where unarmed civilians approached the fence, were met with live fire from IDF snipers — resulting in 214 deaths and over 36,000 injuries.

FIN-2019 // Economic Warfare

Banking & Liquidity Blockade

UNCTAD's 2025 assessment confirmed the total collapse of Gaza's financial system: by April 2025, 98% of banks had ceased operations. This is the culmination of a strategy that began in 2007, when Israel's security cabinet designated Gaza a "hostile entity" and Israeli banks severed all correspondent banking relationships with Gaza institutions (2009). Cash transfers have been suspended by Israel since October 2023. Palestinian banks have no direct access to global financial infrastructure — all international transactions must be routed through Israeli correspondent banks, giving Israel comprehensive veto power over Palestinian economic life. UNCTAD found that trade with Israel constitutes 73% of total Palestinian trade, making the Palestinian economy structurally captive. The Palestinian Economic Policy Research Institute (MAS) documented that the liquidity collapse has enabled traders and money changers to impose extortionate commissions on humanitarian cash distributions. Banking sector exposure to government debt reached $4.7 billion — 40% of total credit.

MED-2020 // Equipment Ban

Radiology & Spare Parts Embargo

Physicians for Human Rights documented that COGAT's dual-use list explicitly classified MRI machines, X-ray tubes, diagnostic imaging scanners, sterilization equipment, surgical instruments, and basic medical device spare parts as controlled items requiring security review. WHO's monitoring confirmed that denied medical equipment persisted as a category throughout the blockade years, with CNN's 2024 investigation specifically identifying X-ray machines among items consistently refused entry as "dual-use." The Lancet's analysis of Gaza's healthcare collapse found that the pre-October 2023 baseline of restricted medical equipment had already degraded the healthcare system's capacity to the point where most oncological and cardiac treatment was structurally impossible within the territory. The WHO's October 2025 monitoring confirmed that WHO-approved supplies, including ICU beds, were stranded at Kerem Shalom while approval was withheld.

EXP-2012 // Trade Suppression

Export Prohibition Directive

UNCTAD's assessments have consistently identified Israel's near-total prohibition on Gazan exports as a key driver of the territory's de-development. At the blockade's peak, Gaza's exports fell by 99%. Goods previously exported to the West Bank and international markets — textiles, furniture, strawberries, flowers, tomatoes, and processed food — were blocked outright. Export prohibition was enforced not through formal legal decree but through COGAT logistical denials, creating a condition of permanent economic paralysis with no actionable legal recourse for Palestinian traders. UNCTAD's November 2025 report found that the Palestinian economy contracted by 35% in 2024 — one of the largest single-year contractions recorded for any economy in modern history — with the export prohibition identified as a structural precondition for that collapse even before October 2023. The Gisha Legal Center documented that the export ban was maintained even during periods when security conditions did not necessitate it, confirming its punitive, non-security purpose.

WTR-2017 // Aquifer Denial

Rainwater & Well Restrictions

In the West Bank, Israeli military orders require Palestinian landowners in Area C — 60% of the West Bank — to obtain permits from the Israeli Civil Administration before constructing new water wells, enlarging existing wells, or installing rainwater harvesting cisterns. OCHA documented that such permits are systematically denied — the same 98% rejection rate applied to building permits broadly in Area C. Structures built without permits, including cisterns and water channels, are subject to demolition orders. Meanwhile, Israeli settlers in the West Bank face no equivalent permit requirement and draw from the same aquifer without restriction. The UN Commission on the Status of Women documented that Palestinian per capita water consumption in the West Bank is approximately 73 litres/day — less than one quarter of Israeli settler consumption at 369 litres/day. In Gaza, the aquifer is 97% polluted and unfit for human consumption due to seawater intrusion — the structural result of over-extraction driven by the prohibition on above-ground water infrastructure.

CIV-2022 // Reconstruction

Cement & Steel Quotas

Following the 2014 Gaza War, international pressure produced the "Gaza Reconstruction Mechanism" (GRM) — a UN-brokered framework designed to allow cement and steel into Gaza for civilian reconstruction under international monitoring. In practice, the GRM subjected every consignment of construction material to a dual Israeli-UN approval process administered via a digitized tracking system. OCHA documented systematic delays, quota restrictions, and arbitrary approvals that throttled reconstruction to a fraction of assessed needs. The UN reported that by 2019 — five years after the 2014 war — reconstruction was only 52% complete. Over 22,000 families who had been displaced since 2014 still had not returned to permanent housing. Gaza's already-damaged housing stock was then further obliterated in operations in 2021 and comprehensively in 2023–2025, producing a total of 320,622 housing units damaged — with 55 million tons of rubble now blocking any reconstruction access.

EDU-2023 // Scholastic Blockade

Educational Material Embargo

COGAT's dual-use list and its opaque administrative enforcement have consistently blocked the entry of educational materials into Gaza. The Guardian's 2024 blockade investigation documented that notebooks and stationery were refused entry at various points. UNRWA reported that its education supply requests — including textbooks, scientific kits, and basic stationery — were routinely subjected to months-long COGAT security review processes that amounted to de facto prohibition. As of October 2023, all of Gaza's 625,000 school-age students lost access to education entirely — UNRWA-run schools converted into displacement shelters. By November 2025, OCHA confirmed that education had been completely suspended for over two years. The UN's assessment found that the pre-war blockade of laboratory equipment had already left Gaza's universities operating without functional science facilities — depriving a generation of students of STEM-equivalent education well before the 2023 escalation.

MED-2025 // Starvation Engineering

Famine as Policy — IPC Phase 5

On 22 August 2025, WHO, FAO, WFP, and UNICEF jointly confirmed famine in the Gaza Strip — the first formal famine declaration issued for the territory. The IPC analysis found that 640,000+ people face IPC Phase 5 (Catastrophe) conditions — marked by starvation, destitution, and preventable death — with the entire 2.1 million population in Crisis or above. The UN Commission of Inquiry found that famine conditions were "the foreseeable and intentional result of Israel's policies," pointing specifically to the aid blocking mechanisms at Kerem Shalom, the destruction of agricultural infrastructure, the fuel embargo, and the systematic dismantling of food production and storage capacity. OCHA's March 2026 data confirmed that nearly all humanitarian aid movements to Kerem Shalom were being denied for consecutive days, with WHO ICU beds and medicine consignments stranded at the terminal. The report explicitly distinguished Gaza's famine from climate-related or conflict-incidental famines: it is bureaucratically administered.

ECON-2024 // Economic De-development

GDP Collapse & Structural Dependency

UNCTAD's November 2025 report recorded that the Palestinian economy contracted by 35% in 2024 — one of the largest single-year contractions for any economy in the post-WWII record. Gaza's GDP contracted by 84% in 2024 alone. UNCTAD's multi-year series on Palestinian economic development found that the cumulative effect of blockade-era restrictions — export prohibition, import restrictions, permit regimes, banking isolation, and movement restrictions — caused the Palestinian economy to contract or stagnate in every year from 2007 to 2024, producing a "deliberate de-development" over a 17-year period. Trade with Israel constitutes 73% of total Palestinian trade — a structural captivity that grants Israel comprehensive economic veto power. UNCTAD concluded that without complete and irreversible lifting of all restrictions and substantial international investment, economic recovery would require decades.

AID-2024 // Obstruction Doctrine

Humanitarian Aid Weaponization

OCHA's March 2026 reporting confirmed that the UN2720 CHI mechanism — the only approved channel for coordinating humanitarian supply movements into Gaza — had an overall approval rate of 55% for requests submitted since April 2024. 111 requests (15%) were explicitly rejected; 215 (29%) remained pending. Cleared supplies may only enter on Tuesdays and Wednesdays, through Kerem Shalom, via the militarized Philadelphi corridor. Between 4–10 February 2025, less than 60% of approved Egyptian consignments could even be offloaded at the crossing. OCHA confirmed that on 12–13 March 2026, nearly all humanitarian movements were denied for two consecutive days — including WHO-approved ICU beds and 170 pallets of essential medicines including paracetamol, aspirin, and ibuprofen. The UN Commission of Inquiry classified systematic aid obstruction as a method of warfare constituting a violation of Article 54 of the Additional Protocol I to the Geneva Conventions.
