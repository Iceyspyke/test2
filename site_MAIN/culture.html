Mosque Eradication

79% of Gaza's Mosques

The Palestinian Ministry of Religious Affairs confirmed that Israeli attacks destroyed 814 out of 1,245 mosques in Gaza — 79% of all religious sites — and partially damaged an additional 151. All three of Gaza's churches were also destroyed. Nineteen of sixty cemeteries were directly targeted, with graves bulldozed, bodies exhumed, and remains crushed by military machinery or transported to unknown locations.

Natural Heritage

Gaza Zoological Network

Euro-Med Human Rights Monitor confirmed that Israeli bombing and systematic starvation destroyed approximately 97% of Gaza's animal wealth. Gaza's 19 zoos, home to 100 exotic and wild species including lions, ostriches, and eagles, were entirely destroyed. The last surviving animals perished in abandoned, bombed facilities. Working animals used as the final means of transport amid fuel shortages were also systematically killed.

Necroviolence

Systematic Cemetery Desecration

CNN satellite analysis confirmed the desecration of at least 16 Palestinian cemeteries during ground operations, with gravestones destroyed, bodies exhumed, and remains transported to unknown destinations. Euro-Med Monitor documented the Israeli army bulldozing the Sheikh Shaban Cemetery in Gaza City between December 17–20, 2024, running over bodies with military machinery and mixing remains to prevent identification — a documented act of necroviolence constituting the erasure of ancestral memory.

West Bank // Academic Isolation

Birzeit University Isolation Order

An Israeli military order titled "Procedure for Entry and Residency of Foreigners in Judea and Samaria Region" grants the military broad powers to deny and revoke visas for international academics contracted to teach at Palestinian universities. Birzeit University, Al-Haq, and Adalah — The Legal Center for Arab Minority Rights — launched formal legal proceedings demanding an end to this policy, documenting a multi-year systematic campaign to isolate Palestinian higher education from the global academic community.

UN Expert Declarations on Scholasticide

Official statements by UN Special Rapporteurs and mandate-holders invoking the term "Scholasticide" as a legal and conceptual designation for the documented destruction of Palestinian education and culture.

UN SPECIAL RAPPORTEURS — JOINT STATEMENTOHCHR // Apr 18, 2024
"The persistent, callous attacks on educational infrastructure in Gaza have a devastating long-term impact on the fundamental rights of people in Gaza... With more than 80% of schools in Gaza damaged or destroyed, it may be reasonable to ask whether this is scholasticide — the deliberate targeting of educational and cultural institutions to eliminate the knowledge, identity, and future of an entire people." — UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights, April 18, 2024.
LSE / ACADEMIC RECORDApr 2024
As of April 2024: over 80% of schools damaged or destroyed. 5,479 students killed. 261 teachers killed. 95 university professors killed. Every single university in the Gaza Strip — 12 institutions — damaged beyond operational capacity or demolished entirely. The term "scholasticide" was formally adopted into UN expert discourse to describe the annihilation of the institutional infrastructure through which a people transmit identity, language, history, and knowledge across generations.

UNESCO's comprehensive post-offensive damage and reconstruction assessment found: 95% of all university campuses affected; 22 of 38 campuses completely destroyed; 195 of 206 buildings destroyed or severely damaged; 120,000 computers and 105 servers obliterated; total education infrastructure damage estimated at $341 million — with reconstruction projected to require decades.
