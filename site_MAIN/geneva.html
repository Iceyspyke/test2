The Geneva Conventions

The baseline legal matrix governing the conduct of armed conflict and prolonged military occupation. The logs below map specific articles against verified actions documented elsewhere in the archive.

Art. 33
4th Convention

Prohibition of Collective Punishment

"No protected person may be punished for an offence he or she has not personally committed. Collective penalties and likewise all measures of intimidation or of terrorism are prohibited." Documented breaches: total blockade of food, fuel, water, and electricity to 2.3 million civilians; blanket evacuation orders applied to entire governorates; indiscriminate destruction of residential infrastructure in retaliation for military operations by non-state actors. OHCHR Thematic Report (June 2024) and UN Special Rapporteurs have specifically cited Art. 33 in the context of the siege architecture imposed on Gaza as a population-level collective punishment measure.

Art. 49
4th Convention

Deportation & Settler Transfer

"Individual or mass forcible transfers, as well as deportations of protected persons from occupied territory to the territory of the Occupying Power or to that of any other country, occupied or not, are prohibited... The Occupying Power shall not deport or transfer parts of its own civilian population into the territory it occupies." Documented breaches: 700,000+ Israeli settlers in the West Bank and East Jerusalem — each individual transfer constituting an Art. 49(6) violation. The International Court of Justice's July 2024 Advisory Opinion explicitly ruled the settlement enterprise unlawful and in violation of Art. 49. The Rome Statute classifies transfer of own civilian population into occupied territory as a war crime under Art. 8(2)(b)(viii).

View ICJ Advisory Opinion
Art. 18
4th Convention

Protection of Civilian Hospitals

"Civilian hospitals organized to give care to the wounded and sick, the infirm and maternity cases, may in no circumstances be the object of attack, but shall at all times be respected and protected by the Parties to the conflict." Documented breaches: Al-Shifa Medical Complex (two military sieges), Kamal Adwan Hospital (tank fire on maternity ward; director detained by name), Nasser Hospital (raid, mass grave discovery), Al-Rantisi Pediatric Hospital (occupied as military outpost), Indonesian Hospital (artillery and sniper fire). Human Rights Watch (March 2025) classified the occupation and destruction of medical facilities as war crimes. WHO confirmed 94% of Gaza's hospitals have been damaged or destroyed. ICRC: Art. 18 protection is absolute and cannot be suspended for any military reason.

View HRW War Crimes Report
Art. 53
4th Convention

Prohibited Destruction of Property

"Any destruction by the Occupying Power of real or personal property belonging individually or collectively to private persons, or to the State, or to other public authorities, or to social or cooperative organizations, is prohibited." Documented breaches: UNOSAT satellite analysis (January 2024) found that 50–70% of all structures in northern Gaza had been damaged or destroyed — the most rapid pace of urban destruction recorded in any conflict since World War II. This includes residential buildings, universities, mosques, churches, cultural heritage sites, archives, printing presses, and civic institutions. The UN Special Rapporteur on Cultural Rights designated the destruction as a deliberate erasure of Palestinian cultural heritage constituting cultural genocide.

View OHCHR Breach Report
Art. 3
Common Article

Minimum Standard of Humanity

Requires humane treatment for all persons in enemy hands, specifically prohibiting "violence to life and person, in particular murder of all kinds, mutilation, cruel treatment and torture" and "outrages upon personal dignity." Documented breaches: mass detention of Palestinian civilians stripped and blindfolded in hospital courtyards; death in custody of Dr. Adnan Al-Bursh (head of orthopaedics, Al-Shifa) confirmed by Palestinian prisoner associations as torture; mass graves at Al-Shifa and Nasser Hospital containing bodies with zip-tied wrists and execution-style wounds; Israeli whistleblower testimony to +972 Magazine and Haaretz documenting systematic abuse at Sde Teiman detention facility. ICRC: Common Article 3 applies in all circumstances and cannot be derogated from by any military necessity claim.

View HRW Detention Report
Art. 51
Protocol I

Indiscriminate Attacks Prohibited

The civilian population as such, as well as individual civilians, shall not be the object of attack. Acts or threats of violence the primary purpose of which is to spread terror among the civilian population are prohibited. Art. 51(5)(b) prohibits attacks where expected civilian casualties are excessive relative to concrete and direct military advantage anticipated. Documented breaches: OHCHR Thematic Report (June 2024) documented 8 specific airstrikes against civilians with no identified military target nearby; the Lavender AI system pre-approved up to 20 civilian deaths per "junior" target kill (confirmed by 6 IDF intelligence officers to +972 Magazine); OHCHR classified multiple strikes as "indiscriminate or disproportionate" under Art. 51 — constituting war crimes. Jurist review (December 2025): IDF's stated 2:1 civilian-to-combatant kill ratio itself triggers proportionality violation triggers under Art. 51(5)(b).

View OHCHR Indiscriminate Attacks Report
Art. 54
Protocol I

Protection of Objects Indispensable to Survival

Starvation of civilians as a method of warfare is prohibited. It is prohibited to attack, destroy, remove or render useless objects indispensable to the survival of the civilian population, such as foodstuffs, agricultural areas, and drinking water installations. Documented breaches: IPC Integrated Food Security Phase Classification (December 2024) found the entire population of Gaza facing acute food insecurity, with 1 in 4 facing catastrophic famine (Phase 5); all 11 of Gaza's water and wastewater treatment plants were destroyed or non-functional by January 2024; agricultural land bombed and bulldozed; fishing access denied. The UN Special Committee on the Situation with Regards to the Implementation of the Declaration on the Granting of Independence to Colonial Countries formally designated starvation as a weapon of war under Art. 54. The ICJ's January 2024 provisional measures specifically ordered Israel to ensure unimpeded humanitarian aid — indicating the Court's assessment of Art. 54 breach risk.

Art. 32
4th Convention

Prohibition of Corporal Punishment & Torture

"The High Contracting Parties specifically agree that each of them is prohibited from taking any measure of such a character as to cause the physical suffering or extermination of protected persons in their hands." Documented breaches: Israeli whistleblowers published testimony in Haaretz (July 2024) describing systematic sexual abuse, electric shocks, stress positions, sleep deprivation, and dog attacks on Palestinian detainees at Sde Teiman and other facilities; Dr. Adnan Al-Bursh tortured to death in Ofer Prison (April 2024, confirmed by prisoner associations); UN Special Rapporteur on Torture formally requested Israel provide information on death-in-custody cases. Al-Haq legal analysis cited Art. 32 among the provisions breached by documented systematic abuse of Palestinian detainees across Israel's detention estate. Art. 32 violations constitute grave breaches of the Convention under Art. 147.

View Al-Haq Legal Analysis
Art. 27
4th Convention

Treatment of Protected Persons

Protected persons are entitled, in all circumstances, to respect for their persons, their honour, their family rights, their religious convictions and practices, and their manners and customs. Documented breaches: mass stripping and public humiliation of Palestinian male detainees at military checkpoints and hospital raids; destruction of mosques, churches, cemeteries, and cultural heritage sites; denial of family notification for detained individuals (Abu Safiya, Al-Bursh); demolition of family homes as punitive measure for actions of family members; the Blue Wolf biometric gamification system, which treats the civilian population as data subjects to be competitively catalogued, constitutes a fundamental violation of the dignity protections embedded in Art. 27. ICRC: Art. 27 encompasses not only physical integrity but full dignitary and personal identity protections without distinction.

View GCIV Text & Analysis
Art. 147
4th Convention

Grave Breaches — Criminal War Crimes

Defines the acts constituting grave breaches — the most serious violations of the Convention — triggering mandatory prosecution obligations for all signatory states: "wilful killing, torture or inhuman treatment, including biological experiments, wilfully causing great suffering or serious injury to body or health, unlawful deportation or transfer or unlawful confinement of a protected person, wilfully depriving a protected person of the rights of fair and regular trial, taking of hostages and extensive destruction and appropriation of property, not justified by military necessity and carried out unlawfully and wantonly." Al-Haq specifically invoked Art. 147 in documenting wilful killings of civilian police at food distribution checkpoints as grave breaches constituting war crimes. Each grave breach triggers universal jurisdiction — any state may, and is obligated to, prosecute or extradite persons responsible regardless of nationality or location of offence.

Art. 55
4th Convention

Duty to Supply Food & Medical Stores

"To the fullest extent of the means available to it, the Occupying Power has the duty of ensuring the food and medical supplies of the population; it should, in particular, bring in the necessary foodstuffs, medical stores and other articles if the resources of the occupied territory are inadequate." Documented breaches: Israel has systemically blocked or delayed food, medical, and fuel aid convoys through Kerem Shalom and Rafah crossings throughout the conflict; MSF field surgeons confirmed performing amputations without general anaesthetic due to blocked supply chains; OHCHR confirmed the blockade "prevented the entry of objects indispensable to the survival of civilians." The ICRC Practical Guide to Humanitarian Law states Art. 55 creates a positive obligation — the Occupying Power must actively source and deliver supplies, not merely refrain from blocking them.

View ICRC Analysis
Art. 56
4th Convention

Hygiene & Public Health in Occupied Territory

"To the fullest extent of the means available to it, the Occupying Power has the duty of ensuring and maintaining, with the cooperation of national and local authorities, the medical and hospital establishments and services, public health and hygiene in the occupied territory." Documented breaches: all 11 of Gaza's water and wastewater treatment plants rendered non-functional by January 2024; raw sewage flooding displacement camps; WHO-confirmed cVDPV2 polio epidemic — the first in Gaza in 25 years — directly caused by WASH infrastructure destruction; hepatitis A, diarrheal diseases, and scabies now endemic in displacement camps. ICRC: Art. 56 obligations apply regardless of military necessity and independently of whether the occupied territory's infrastructure existed before the conflict.

View ICRC Health Law Factsheet
Art. 23
4th Convention

Free Passage of Essential Supplies

Requires each High Contracting Party to allow the free passage of all consignments of medicine, medical material, and foodstuffs intended solely for civilians of another party, even if that party is an adversary. Documented breaches: repeated closure and restriction of Kerem Shalom, Rafah, and Erez crossings; documented instances of aid trucks turned back mid-convoy; flour and food convoys looted or blocked; the ICJ's January 26, 2024 provisional measures order specifically instructed Israel to "take effective measures to ensure the unimpeded access of investigators" and humanitarian aid — a direct signal of Art. 23 breach risk. OHCHR February 2024: "The scale of denial of access to humanitarian assistance is unprecedented in recent history."

View ICRC Aid Access Analysis
Art. 57
Protocol I

Precautions in Attack

Requires commanders to "do everything feasible to verify that the objectives to be attacked are neither civilians nor civilian objects," to "take all feasible precautions in the choice of means and methods of attack," and to cancel any attack "which may be expected to cause incidental loss of civilian life... which would be excessive in relation to the concrete and direct military advantage anticipated." Documented breaches: +972 Magazine confirmed IDF officers reviewed Lavender-generated targets for as little as 20 seconds before authorizing lethal strikes; "Where's Daddy?" by design triggers strikes when civilians are present in highest numbers; HRW documented multiple airstrikes against crowds gathering at aid distribution points with no prior warning or military target identification. OHCHR Thematic Report (June 2024): multiple strikes documented as failing Art. 57 feasibility precaution requirements.

View OHCHR Precautions Report
Art. 58
Protocol I

Precautions Against Effects of Attacks

Requires parties to "endeavour to remove the civilian population, individual civilians and civilian objects under their control from the vicinity of military objectives," and to "avoid locating military objectives within or near densely populated areas." Documented breaches: IDF operated military headquarters and command posts within and beneath civilian infrastructure (including Al-Shifa Hospital compound — the primary justification given for the hospital siege); issued evacuation orders to "safe zones" which were subsequently themselves struck; directed civilians toward Rafah and then conducted large-scale Rafah operations. OHCHR: issuing mass evacuation orders that direct civilians into subsequently attacked zones does not constitute compliance with Art. 58 precaution obligations, and does not transfer legal responsibility for civilian deaths to victims who followed official instructions.

View OHCHR Precautions Report
Art. 20
4th Convention

Protection of Medical Personnel

"Persons regularly and solely engaged in the operation and administration of civilian hospitals, including the personnel engaged in the search for, removal and transporting of and caring for wounded and sick civilians... shall be respected and protected." Documented breaches: Dr. Adnan Al-Bursh — head of orthopaedics, Al-Shifa — detained and tortured to death in Ofer Prison; Dr. Hussam Abu Safiya — director, Kamal Adwan — detained by name from active facility; 6+ senior medical specialists killed or disappeared in custody (HRW, March 2025); PRCS paramedics Yusuf al-Zeino and Ahmed al-Madhoun killed while responding to the Hind Rajab rescue dispatch under COGAT military clearance (Forensic Architecture, 2024). ICRC: Art. 20 protection applies to all medical personnel at all times, regardless of the party's military assessment of the facility or broader context.

View HRW Medical Personnel Report
Art. 1
Common Article

Third-State Obligation to Ensure Respect

"The High Contracting Parties undertake to respect and to ensure respect for the present Convention in all circumstances." Common Article 1 establishes a positive obligation for all 196 signatory states — not merely the parties to a conflict — to ensure IHL is respected. This is the foundational basis for third-state arms embargo obligations, diplomatic action, and ICJ proceedings. The ICJ confirmed in Nicaragua v. United States (1986) and Military and Paramilitary Activities that Common Article 1 creates binding obligations on all states not to encourage violations by others. OHCHR (February 2024) specifically invoked Common Article 1 in its call on Germany, the United States, the United Kingdom, Italy, Belgium, the Netherlands, Canada, and Japan to immediately cease arms transfers to Israel — confirming each named state's active breach of its Convention-based obligations.
