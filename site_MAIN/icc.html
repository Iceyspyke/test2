International Criminal Court

Applications for Warrants of Arrest // Situation in the State of Palestine

Formal applications filed by ICC Prosecutor Karim Khan regarding reasonable grounds to believe senior state officials bear criminal responsibility for war crimes and crimes against humanity committed in the State of Palestine. Case reference: ICC-01/18. All 125 ICC member states are legally obligated to arrest and surrender any subject who enters their territory — no exception exists under the Rome Statute for heads of state or government officials of non-member states.

Warrant Issued ICC-01/18-A
Benjamin Netanyahu
Prime Minister of Israel
  • Starvation of civilians as a method of warfare (War Crime — Art. 8(2)(b)(xxv))
  • Wilfully causing great suffering or serious injury to body or health (War Crime)
  • Extermination and/or murder (Crime Against Humanity — Art. 7(1)(b)/(a))
  • Intentionally directing attacks against a civilian population (War Crime)
  • Persecution on political, national, ethnic, and/or religious grounds (Crime Against Humanity)
  • Other inhumane acts — forcible transfer of population (Crime Against Humanity)

Warrant issued 21 November 2024 by Pre-Trial Chamber I (Judges Guillou, Alapini-Gansou, Hohler). Chamber found reasonable grounds to believe Netanyahu co-perpetrated the war crime of starvation as method of warfare and crimes against humanity as part of a widespread and systematic attack against the civilian population of Gaza. Israel's jurisdictional challenges were unanimously rejected. On 2–6 April 2025, Netanyahu travelled to Hungary — an ICC member state — and was not arrested, constituting Hungary's first documented breach of Article 89 surrender obligations.

Warrant Issued ICC-01/18-B
Yoav Gallant
Former Minister of Defence of Israel
  • Starvation of civilians as a method of warfare (War Crime — Art. 8(2)(b)(xxv))
  • Wilfully causing great suffering or serious injury to body or health (War Crime)
  • Extermination and/or murder (Crime Against Humanity — Art. 7(1)(b)/(a))
  • Intentionally directing attacks against a civilian population (War Crime)
  • Persecution on political, national, ethnic, and/or religious grounds (Crime Against Humanity)
  • Other inhumane acts — forcible transfer of population (Crime Against Humanity)

Warrant issued 21 November 2024. Gallant served as Minister of Defence from October 2022 until his dismissal by Netanyahu on 5 November 2024 — 16 days before the warrants were issued. The Chamber found that Gallant, jointly with Netanyahu, restricted the entry of humanitarian assistance including food, water, medicine, fuel, and electricity into Gaza, thereby co-perpetrating the war crime of starvation. His removal from office does not affect the warrant — the Rome Statute provides no immunity for former officials. Gallant has not been arrested as of April 2026.

The Guardian reported on 28 April 2025, citing court officials familiar with the investigation, that Prosecutor Khan is preparing a new round of warrant applications against Israeli suspects. Judges issued a sealed order preventing Khan from publicly acknowledging the existence of the applications. The Westpoint Lieber Institute and ECCHR have noted the Court cannot exclude that some requests have already been submitted and are being kept confidential under Article 57(3)(c) of the Rome Statute.

ICJ Case — Pending Nicaragua v. Germany
Federal Republic of Germany
Respondent State Party // ICJ Case No. 193
  • Facilitation of genocide through continued military exports to Israel (Genocide Convention, Art. I)
  • Defunding of UNRWA humanitarian operations in January 2024 (violation of humanitarian obligations)
  • Failure to prevent violations of the Genocide Convention (Art. I — obligation to prevent)
  • Failure to ensure respect for international humanitarian law (Geneva Convention Common Art. 1)

Nicaragua filed the application on 1 March 2024, two days after Germany approved a new arms export license to Israel. Germany suspended major weapons deliveries shortly after the case was filed. The ICJ declined provisional measures in April 2024, ruling that Germany had already changed course. The case remains on the docket as a test of third-state complicity obligations under the Genocide Convention — with implications for all arms-supplying states including the US, UK, and Italy. Germany has since resumed partial arms deliveries, complicating the case posture.

ICJ Case — Active South Africa v. Israel
State of Israel
Respondent State // ICJ Case — Application of the Genocide Convention
  • Genocide — acts committed with intent to destroy the Palestinian people in whole or in part (Genocide Convention Art. II)
  • Failure to prevent genocide (Art. I)
  • Incitement to genocide by senior Israeli officials (Art. III)
  • Non-compliance with Court-ordered provisional measures (January 26, 2024)
  • Deliberate obstruction of humanitarian aid in violation of provisional measures order

South Africa filed on 29 December 2023. On 26 January 2024, the ICJ issued provisional measures 15–2 — ordering Israel to take all measures within its power to prevent acts under Art. II of the Genocide Convention, ensure humanitarian access, preserve evidence, and submit a compliance report by 23 February 2024. The Court declined to order a suspension of military operations but found South Africa's rights under the Convention "plausible." The case now proceeds to the merits phase, which could take years. South Africa filed updated submissions in 2025 citing continued non-compliance with provisional measures. Canada, Colombia, Mexico, the Netherlands, Nicaragua, Belgium, Spain, and Ireland have formally declared they are joining or supporting the proceedings.

Non-Compliance Documented ASP Referral — Hungary
Hungary
ICC Member State — Article 87(7) Non-Compliance
  • Invited Netanyahu for official state visit on 2–6 April 2025 — first visit by a warrant subject to an ICC member state
  • Failed to arrest and surrender Netanyahu under Art. 89(1) of the Rome Statute
  • Assembly of States Parties (ASP) sent formal reminder of obligations on 31 March 2025 — Hungary proceeded regardless
  • Under Art. 87(7), the Court may refer non-complying states to the ASP or UN Security Council for further action
  • Sets first formal precedent for an ICC member state openly defying a warrant for a serving head of government

Hungary's non-compliance is the first documented instance of an ICC member state willfully refusing to enforce an outstanding warrant. LPHR legal briefing (January 2026, updated August 2025) documents Hungary's obligations and failure in detail. The incident has forced legal clarification from multiple ICC member states on their own enforcement obligations. France, Germany, the UK, and the Netherlands have confirmed in writing that they would be legally obligated to arrest Netanyahu if he entered their territory — no executive discretion exists under Art. 27 of the Rome Statute, which removes immunity for all officials including heads of state.

Procedure Reference
ROME STATUTE

Court Jurisdiction & Legal Basis

ICC Case No. ICC-01/18 // Situation in the State of Palestine
  • Palestine acceded to the Rome Statute on 2 January 2015, granting the ICC jurisdiction over crimes committed in Palestinian territory from 13 June 2014 onward.
  • Pre-Trial Chamber I confirmed territorial jurisdiction on 5 February 2021 — 5 judges in favour, 1 dissenting — finding the Court's jurisdiction extends to Gaza, the West Bank, and East Jerusalem.
  • Formal investigation opened 3 March 2021 by Prosecutor Fatou Bensouda.
  • Warrant applications filed 20 May 2024 by Prosecutor Karim Khan for 5 individuals.
  • Israel's Art. 18 complementarity challenge and Art. 19 jurisdiction challenge both unanimously rejected by Pre-Trial Chamber I on 21 November 2024.
  • Warrants issued 21 November 2024 for Netanyahu, Gallant, and Deif. Deif proceedings terminated 26 February 2025.
  • Art. 27 of the Rome Statute: official capacity — including Head of State — is irrelevant and does not constitute grounds for reduction of sentence or exemption from prosecution.
  • All 125 ICC member states are legally obligated to arrest any subject who enters their territory under Art. 89(1). No exceptions exist.

Prosecution Timeline

29 NOV 2012
UN General Assembly Resolution 67/19 grants Palestine non-member observer state status — enabling accession to international treaties including the Rome Statute.
02 JAN 2015
Palestine formally accedes to the Rome Statute, granting the ICC jurisdiction over crimes committed in Palestinian territory retroactively from 13 June 2014.
05 FEB 2021
Pre-Trial Chamber I rules 5-1 that the ICC's territorial jurisdiction extends to Gaza, the West Bank, and East Jerusalem — over Israeli objections and a US State Department condemnation.
03 MAR 2021
Formal investigation opened into "Situation in the State of Palestine" by Prosecutor Fatou Bensouda — covering crimes committed by all parties in Gaza, the West Bank, and East Jerusalem.
29 DEC 2023
South Africa files Application of the Genocide Convention against Israel at the ICJ. The filing cites a pattern of genocidal statements by senior Israeli officials and systematic destruction of civilian infrastructure as evidence of genocidal intent.
26 JAN 2024
ICJ issues provisional measures 15–2 in South Africa v. Israel: Israel ordered to prevent acts under the Genocide Convention, ensure humanitarian access, preserve evidence, and submit a compliance report by 23 February 2024. Israel does not comply with the aid access measure.
20 MAY 2024
Prosecutor Karim Khan publicly announces applications for arrest warrants for Netanyahu, Gallant, Sinwar, Haniyeh, and Deif — the first joint application simultaneously targeting leaders on both sides of an active conflict. The US threatens sanctions against the Court in response.
09 AUG 2024
ICC Pre-Trial Chamber grants Prosecution withdrawal of Haniyeh warrant application following his assassination in Tehran on 31 July 2024. The killing is attributed to Israeli intelligence.
25 OCT 2024
ICC Pre-Trial Chamber grants withdrawal of Sinwar warrant application following his confirmed death in an IDF raid in Rafah on 16 October 2024.
21 NOV 2024
Pre-Trial Chamber I issues arrest warrants for Netanyahu, Gallant, and Deif after unanimously rejecting Israel's Art. 18 complementarity challenge and Art. 19 jurisdiction challenge. This is the first time the ICC has issued a warrant for a sitting head of government of a US-allied Western state.
26 FEB 2025
ICC formally terminates proceedings against Mohammed Deif following Hamas confirmation of his death on 30 January 2025. Two of the five original warrant subjects are now deceased; three warrants remain active.
02–06 APR 2025
Benjamin Netanyahu travels to Hungary for a state visit — the first visit by a warrant subject to an ICC member state. Hungary does not arrest him. The Assembly of States Parties had issued a formal reminder of Hungary's Art. 89 obligations on 31 March 2025. Hungary's non-compliance sets the first explicit precedent for state defiance of an active ICC warrant.
28 APR 2025
The Guardian reports that ICC judges have issued a sealed gag order barring Prosecutor Khan from publicly acknowledging new warrant applications in the Palestine case. Khan is understood to be preparing a new round of applications against Israeli suspects, with a focus on crimes in the West Bank. No official public confirmation is possible under the order.
ONGOING // 2026
125 ICC member states legally obligated to enforce the Netanyahu and Gallant warrants. France, Germany, the UK, the Netherlands, and Belgium have confirmed their obligation in writing. Hungary has documented its refusal. Israel denies ICC jurisdiction and has refused all cooperation. The South Africa v. Israel genocide case continues at the ICJ merits phase. A new round of sealed warrant applications for additional Israeli suspects is believed to be under consideration by Pre-Trial Chamber I.
