Procedure Reference
ROME STATUTE

Court Jurisdiction

ICC Case No. ICC-01/18
  • Palestine acceded to the Rome Statute on 2 Jan 2015, granting the ICC jurisdiction.
  • The Prosecutor opened a formal investigation on 3 March 2021.
  • Warrant applications were filed 20 May 2024.
  • ICC Pre-Trial Chamber I issued warrants for Netanyahu and Gallant on 21 November 2024.
  • All 124 ICC member states are legally obligated to arrest any subject who enters their territory.

Prosecution Timeline

03 MAR 2021
Formal Investigation opened into "Situation in the State of Palestine" by Prosecutor Fatou Bensouda.
20 MAY 2024
Prosecutor Karim Khan applies for arrest warrants for Netanyahu, Gallant, and senior Hamas leaders including Sinwar, Haniyeh, and Deif.
21 NOV 2024
Pre-Trial Chamber I issues arrest warrants for Netanyahu and Gallant. This is the first time the ICC has issued a warrant for a sitting head of government of a close Western ally.
ONGOING
124 member states legally obligated to execute warrants. Several nations including France, Germany, and the UK confirm legal obligation to arrest. Israel denies ICC jurisdiction.
