WEST BANK OBSTACLE SURVEY — OCHA RAPID ASSESSMENTJAN–FEB 2025
A swift assessment carried out by OCHA during January and February 2025 revealed that there are presently 849 barriers permanently or intermittently hindering the movement of 3.3 million Palestinians throughout the West Bank, including East Jerusalem and the H2 sector of Hebron. This marks a significant escalation: in September 2024, OCHA recorded 793 obstacles, itself already representing 86 new barriers added since 7 October 2023. The breakdown includes 89 checkpoints staffed 24/7, 149 partial checkpoints (46 with gates), 158 earthmounds, 196 road gates (122 of which are usually closed), 104 road blocks, and 97 linear closures each blocking one or more roads. Since 7 October 2023, movement restrictions on 3.3 million Palestinians have only tightened. Full OCHA Report →
KEREM SHALOM — AID DENIAL TELEMETRYSOURCE: OCHA // Mar 13, 2026
As of 13 March 2026, OCHA confirmed that nearly all humanitarian movements to the Kerem Shalom crossing — the only remaining operational entry point into Gaza — were denied for two consecutive days. Only fuel was collected. WHO supplies stranded at the crossing include nearly 50 ICU beds and 170 pallets of medicines including paracetamol, aspirin and ibuprofen. The Rafah and Zikim crossings have been shut since the start of the regional escalation on 28 February 2026. Of 732 Coordination of Humanitarian Inputs (CHI) requests submitted under the UN2720 mechanism since April 2024, only 406 (55%) have been approved, 111 (15%) rejected, and 215 (29%) remain pending. Between 4–10 February 2025, less than 60% of consignments from Egypt could be offloaded at Kerem Shalom. Supplies cleared for entry are permitted exclusively on Tuesdays and Wednesdays. Bureaucratic Control Mechanisms

MECHANISM: PERMIT ARCHITECTURE

The 100+ Permit System

The Israeli Civil Administration (COGAT) administers a labyrinth of over 100 distinct permit categories required for Palestinians to travel, build, work, access land, receive medical care, or attend religious sites. Permits are individually processed, frequently denied without stated reason, revocable without notice, and expire on arbitrary timelines. The system structurally guarantees that normal civic life requires perpetual bureaucratic surveillance. Approval rates for medical permits average below 40%. Work permit approvals collapsed from 130,000+ active permits to near-zero for Gaza workers following 7 October 2023.

MECHANISM: THE SEPARATION BARRIER

712 km Wall — Seam Zone Regime

The International Court of Justice ruled in its July 2004 Advisory Opinion that the construction of the Separation Wall in the West Bank is contrary to international law, and that Israel is under an obligation to dismantle it. As of 2025, the 712 km barrier — 85% of which runs inside the West Bank rather than along the Green Line — remains the largest single movement obstacle. It isolates 9.4% of West Bank land and the entirety of East Jerusalem from the Palestinian population. The Seam Zone created by the Wall requires Palestinians with land behind it to hold "permanent resident" status confirmed by additional permits simply to reach their own farmland. 74 agricultural gates are the only access points — open at arbitrary, infrequent hours.

MECHANISM: HUMANITARIAN ACCESS CONTROLS

UN2720 Mechanism — Aid Gatekeeping

The UN2720 Coordination of Humanitarian Inputs (CHI) mechanism, established April 2024, requires UN agencies and international NGOs to submit formal requests to Israeli authorities before moving any designated humanitarian supply into Gaza. Out of 732 requests submitted by December 2024, only 406 (55%) were approved — 111 (15%) were explicitly rejected and 215 (29%) remained pending. Cleared items enter Gaza exclusively on Tuesdays and Wednesdays, only through Kerem Shalom. All cargo is subsequently routed through the militarized Philadelphi corridor. Shipments from Jordan are restricted to routes requiring multiple offloading and reloading points. Between 4–10 February 2025, less than 60% of Egypt consignments could even be offloaded at the crossing.

MECHANISM: OPERATION IRON WALL

West Bank Refugee Camp Occupation

On 21 January 2025 — two days after the Gaza ceasefire took effect — the Israeli military launched Operation Iron Wall in the northern West Bank. Beginning in Jenin, the operation expanded to the refugee camps of Tulkarm and Nur Shams. All three camps were forcibly evacuated. Israeli forces have been in continuous occupation since. The Israeli Minister of Defence instructed the military to prepare for a "long stay" of up to one year. 162 demolition orders have been issued for structures in Jenin camp; 241 for Tulkarm and Nur Shams combined. Residents were notified via Palestinian District Liaison Offices to retrieve belongings before demolition. No return date has been announced for over 40,000 displaced camp residents.

MECHANISM: CORRIDOR ARCHITECTURE

Netzarim & Philadelphi — Permanent Division

The UN Commission of Inquiry confirmed in its August 2025 report that the Netzarim corridor — a military road bisecting Gaza — saw 95% of all structures within it demolished, including entire neighbourhoods (Juhr al-Dik, Al Mughraqa, Al-Zahra). The Philadelphi corridor along the Gaza-Egypt border was expanded by UNOSAT-confirmed data from 1.5 km² to approximately 19 km² — consuming 5% of Gaza's total land area — with 99% of structures within the expanded zone affected. Human Rights Watch confirmed that deliberate, controlled demolitions in both corridors demonstrate an intention to permanently prevent displaced Palestinians from returning. The Israeli PM listed retention of both corridors as non-negotiable "red lines" in ceasefire talks.

MECHANISM: AREA C PLANNING REGIME

Administrative Dispossession

Area C — covering 60% of the West Bank — is under full Israeli military and civil control. Following occupation in 1967, Palestinian planning committees were dissolved by military order and replaced by the Israeli Higher Planning Council, staffed exclusively by Israeli officers. Between 2009 and 2018, 98% of Palestinian building permit applications were rejected. Between 2016–2020, the rejection rate reached 99%. Palestinians who build without permits face demolition orders and displacement. By contrast, Israeli settlement outline plans approved by authorities cover at least 8.5% of Area C. In 2019–2020 alone, permits for 2,233 settlement housing units were issued. Only 5 of over 100 Palestinian outline plans for Area C have ever received approval.
